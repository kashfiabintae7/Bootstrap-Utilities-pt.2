Famous Authors Who Write Disturbing And Creepy Thrillers

Gillian Flynn

A defining voice in psychological thrillers, Flynn's Gone Girl reshaped the genre with its dark tone, unreliable narration, and gender-focused themes. Other works include Sharp Objects and Dark Places. Her influence is foundational in contemporary suspense fiction.

Tana French

Celebrated for her literary depth and psychological nuance, French’s Dublin Murder Squad novels—In the Woods, The Likeness, and Faithful Place—are critically acclaimed for elevating crime fiction into character-driven psychological studies.

Ruth Ware

Ware’s atmospheric suspense novels have reached international bestseller status. Notable titles include The Woman in Cabin 10, The Turn of the Key, The Lying Game, and One by One She is a modern mainstay in the genre.

Riley Sager

One of the most commercially successful thriller authors of the last decade, Sager’s books—Final Girls, The Last Time I Lied, Home Before Dark, The House Across the Lakeblend horror and suspense, earning him a loyal readership and frequent bestseller status.

Lucy Foley

A leader in the modern "locked-room" subgenre, Foley’s multi-perspective narratives and immersive settings are showcased in The Guest List, The Hunting Party, and The Paris Apartment, making her a global bestseller and critical favorite.

Lisa Jewell

With a background in contemporary fiction, Jewell has successfully transitioned into psychological thrillers. Her most popular titles include The Family Upstairs, Then She Was Gone, Invisible Girl, and None of This Is True.

Alex Michaelides

Michaelides achieved rapid success with The Silent Patient, one of the best-selling debut thrillers of recent years. His follow-ups, The Maidens and The Fury, further established his reputation for psychological insight and narrative twists.

Freida McFadden

A rising figure in domestic thrillers, McFadden’s novels—he Housemaid, The Housemaid’s Secret, Never Lie, and The Coworker—have gained significant popularity. While less critically covered, her commercial success and reader engagement are notable.

B.A. Paris

Paris is widely recognized for her domestic thrillers. Her accessible style and high-stakes narratives are evident in Behind Closed Doors, The Breakdown, Bring Me Back, and The Therapist.

Megan Miranda

Known for atmospheric pacing and nonlinear storytelling, Miranda’s thrillers include All the Missing Girls, The Last House Guest, Such a Quiet Place, and The Only Survivors. Her work earns strong sales and a dedicated following.

Karen M. McManus

Though she writes primarily for a young adult audience, McManus’s intricate plotting and crossover appeal are clear in One of Us Is Lying, One of Us Is Next, Two Can Keep a Secret, and You'll Be the Death of Me.

Shari Lapena

Lapena specializes in fast-paced domestic suspense. Her bestselling titles include The Couple Next Door, A Stranger in the House, An Unwanted Guest, and Not a Happy Family.

Alice Feeney

Feeney’s strength lies in her signature narrative misdirection and layered psychological themes. Her key works include Sometimes I Lie, His & Hers, Rock Paper Scissors, and Daisy Darker.

S.A. Cosby

Critically acclaimed for his gritty, Southern noir thrillers, Cosby's novels include Blacktop Wasteland, Razorblade Tears, and All the Sinners Bleed. He blends action with social commentary, earning high praise in literary and genre circles.

Holly Jackson

Holly Jackson is a British author best known for A Good Girl’s Guide to Murder, which launched a bestselling young adult thriller series. Her work is praised for intricate plotting, morally complex characters, and a true-crime-inspired narrative style that appeals to both teen and adult audiences.
